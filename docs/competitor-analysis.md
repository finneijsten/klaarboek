# KlaarBoek — Competitor Analysis

## Dutch ZZP Bookkeeping Market (2026)

### Moneybird
- **Pricing:** Free tier (10 transactions/month), paid plans €14-33/month
- **Strengths:** Auto-categorizes bank transactions (80-90% accuracy), also offers business bank accounts, strong brand recognition
- **Weaknesses:** Free tier is very limited, still requires manual review of 10-20% of transactions
- **Users:** Largest player in the Dutch ZZP market

### e-Boekhouden.nl
- **Pricing:** 15 months free for starters, then competitive monthly pricing
- **Strengths:** Popular with beginners, extra user included, affordable
- **Weaknesses:** Less automation, more manual entry
- **Users:** Popular among starters and cost-conscious ZZP'ers

### Informer (InformerOnline)
- **Pricing:** 8 packages from ZZP Basis (free first year for new KvK members) to enterprise
- **Strengths:** Wide range of packages, non-profit options
- **Weaknesses:** Complex pricing structure, can be overwhelming
- **Users:** Broader market including MKB and non-profits

### Jortt
- **Pricing:** Free basic plan, paid plans available
- **Strengths:** Simple interface, aimed at non-financial ZZP'ers
- **Weaknesses:** Limited features for growing businesses

## Market Opportunity

- 1.2 million ZZP'ers in the Netherlands
- Most still use an accountant (€500-2000/year) for basic admin
- Existing tools still require significant manual work
- No player has achieved true end-to-end automation including BTW filing

## KlaarBoek Positioning

**"Boekhouding die gewoon werkt."**

- One plan: €9.99/month — undercut the market
- Automatic categorization with transparent rules — you stay in control
- Bank CSV → categorised → quarterly BTW report, ready to file
- Dutch-first: built specifically for the Dutch tax system
